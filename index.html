software gardener

I am a recent college grad, getting my BS in computer science from Georgia Tech, where I specialized in artificial intelligence and computer systems. I worked there as a TA for our undergraduate artificial intelligence course and previously discrete math. Now I am a west coast tech-for-tech guy having previously been a tech-for-finance engineer type on the east coast.

Recently I have started really getting into indie games, the genre is especially interesting to me. A stark contrast to typical "mainstream" games that I am more used to. It is amazing to me to see what a handful (if even!) of people can do in terms of technology, music, and storytelling.

natural language processing

My interests in natural language processing (NLP) began in the summer of 2011, working with some NLP tools while interning with JPMorgan. The following semester I took an introductory course to AI, CS 3600, where I not only learned more about NLP but eventually led me to starting research under Dr. Ai. My research focuses on the analysis of "hot topics" in social networks using NLP techniques.

strong induction

First time undergraduate CS TAs are required to present an artifact, a project or contribution to the class they are teaching. As I teach a proofs and discrete math course, I created a postage problem generator that tested one's knowledge for completing strong induction proofs. It is written in Java; it can be run here in browser or it can be downloaded as an executable jar here.
